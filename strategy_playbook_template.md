## 📧 Email Frameworks (Approved)

### 1. Mouse Trap (First Touch)
**Goal:** Hook the reader with a hyper-relevant question that implies we have the solution.
> **Structure:** [Personalized Observation] → [Binary Problem Question].
> **Example:** "Saw you're expanding the team in Mumbai — curious if you've explored AI screening to handle the initial resume volume?"

### 2. Vanilla Ice Cream (First Touch - Context Heavy)
**Goal:** Provide a bit more context without being too salesy.
> **Structure:** [Observation] → [Problem Insight] → [Social Proof] → [What we do] → [Soft Ask].
> **Example:** "Noticed [Company] recently launched [Product]. Usually this means an influx of support tickets. We helped [Customer] handle their launch volume with AI agents. Worth a chat?"

### 3. Neutral Insight (Follow-up)
**Goal:** Bump the thread while providing value.
> **Structure:** [Industry Trend/Insight] → [How it affects them] → [Soft Ask].

### 4. Thoughtful Bump (Follow-up 2)
**Goal:** Quick check-in.
> **Structure:** 1-2 sentences. "Any feedback on the previous note?"

### 5. Breakup (Final Touch)
**Goal:** Close the loop, invite future contact.
> **Structure:** "Assuming timing is off. Closing the loop for now. Reach out if anything changes."

## 📝 Rules Discovered (Do NOT violate)

These rules were discovered organically by the Observer loop analyzing our sends.

- **Length:** Keep all initial touches under 50 words.
- **Tone:** Use a casual, unsure tone ("Not sure if...", "Curious if..."). Do NOT use "I am writing to..." or "I hope this email finds you well."
- **Formatting:** No bolding, no italics, no bullet points in cold emails. Treat it like a text to a friend.
- **Subject Lines:** 3-6 words, lowercase, internal-sounding. e.g., "handling support volume", "quick question re: your tech stack".
- **Call to Action (CTA):** End with a soft question ("Worth a chat?", "Open to learning more?"). Do NOT ask for "15 minutes on Tuesday."
