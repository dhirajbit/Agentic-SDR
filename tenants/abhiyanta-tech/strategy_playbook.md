## Core Value Proposition

> Abhiyanta Tech is a full-stack SAP partner — implementation, AMS, upgrades, and migrations — for mid-market and enterprise companies running (or moving to) SAP.

### What makes us different
- Coverage across the entire SAP stack — S/4HANA, ECC, Business One, BTP, SuccessFactors, Ariba — not just one module.
- AMS + implementation under one roof, so the team that builds it also runs it.
- Vertical depth in manufacturing, pharma, FMCG, automotive, chemicals.

### Key proof points
- Multi-vertical SAP implementation experience.
- Defined-SLA AMS for L1/L2/L3 production support.
- Migration practice for ECC → S/4HANA (on-prem and RISE with SAP).

## Industry & Role Targeting

### Target 1: Manufacturing / Process / Discrete (mid-market & enterprise)
- **Ideal titles:** CIO, Head of IT, Head of SAP, SAP CoE Lead, Director of Digital Transformation, ERP Manager, IT Director.
- **Pain points:** ECC 2027 deadline, legacy customisations blocking upgrade, expensive incumbent AMS, slow ticket SLAs, post go-live stabilisation.
- **Hook:** "How are you thinking about the ECC 2027 cutover — greenfield S/4 or brownfield?"
- **Framework:** Mouse Trap (first touch), Neutral Insight (follow-up).

### Target 2: Pharma / Chemicals (regulated)
- **Ideal titles:** CIO, Head of IT, Digital / IT Transformation Lead, SAP Validation Lead.
- **Pain points:** Validation overhead on every change, GxP-compliant AMS scarcity, multi-plant rollouts.
- **Hook:** "Curious how your team is handling GxP-validated AMS — most providers we talk to are stretched on this."
- **Framework:** Vanilla Ice Cream.

### Target 3: FMCG / Retail / Distribution
- **Ideal titles:** CIO, VP IT, Head of ERP, Supply Chain IT Lead.
- **Pain points:** Demand-volume spikes (peak season tickets), multi-entity rollouts after M&A, integrations (Ariba, e-commerce).
- **Hook:** "Saw [Company] is expanding into [region/category] — how's the SAP rollout side of that going?"
- **Framework:** Mouse Trap.

## Email Frameworks (approved)

### 1. Mouse Trap (first touch)
> **Structure:** [Personalised observation about their SAP estate / recent news] → [Binary problem question].
> **Example:** "Noticed you're still on ECC — curious if the 2027 cutover is on the roadmap yet, or still in 'we'll figure it out' mode?"

### 2. Vanilla Ice Cream (first touch, context heavy)
> **Structure:** [Observation] → [SAP-specific pain] → [Social proof] → [What we do] → [Soft ask].
> **Example:** "Saw [Company] recently went live on S/4. Usually means a flood of P1/P2 tickets the first 90 days. We run AMS for a few firms in [vertical] in the post-go-live window. Worth a quick chat?"

### 3. Neutral Insight (follow-up 1)
> **Structure:** [SAP industry trend — RISE, 2027 deadline, BTP] → [How it affects them] → [Soft ask].

### 4. Thoughtful Bump (follow-up 2)
> "Any thoughts on the note above? Happy to ghost if not relevant."

### 5. Breakup (final touch)
> "Assuming timing's off — closing the loop. Reach out if the SAP roadmap shifts."

---

## Rules (Do NOT violate)

- **Length:** First touches under 50 words.
- **Tone:** Casual, unsure ("Not sure if...", "Curious if..."). Never "I hope this email finds you well."
- **Formatting:** Plain text. No bold, italics, bullets in first touches.
- **Subject lines:** 3-6 words, lowercase. Examples: "ecc 2027 question", "sap ams pricing", "quick s/4 question".
- **CTA:** Soft question ("Worth a chat?", "Open to a quick call?"). Never "15 min on Tuesday at 3 PM".
- **Never mention competitors by name** (Infosys, TCS, Wipro, Accenture, LTIMindtree, etc.) — even to differentiate.
- **Phone numbers:** We do NOT have direct-dial credit on Apollo. Never request phone reveals.
